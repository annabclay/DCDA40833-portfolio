Reflection

Design Inspiration

For my basemap design, I wanted to use the colors of one of my favorite paintings, "Starry Night". I saved the image, then, when adding colors to my map and its elements, I used the color dropper to pick up those colors. When you do a full zoom out of the map, the water still looks blue, and the land is yellow. I tried to keep the map's mood the same as the painting's. When picking out my fonts, I tried to find some of the most fun and free fonts I could find, because that is what I thought of for the painting. I kept some of the fonts in the original font the website provided, so that so many overwhelming fonts wouldn't take away from the map. When making this map, I wanted to pick a place that was special to me: my hometown. I picked my favorite Lexington staples to add to the map.

Cartographic Challenges

Some of the main challenges I faced when designing my basemap for multiple scales were figuring out how to show only Lexington, not the entire globe. I was very confused at first about how to change the map's view, but then I looked up a tutorial on the website and found out how to do it.

Technical Challenges

One of the technical challenges I faced when creating my interactive map was how to navigate the website. Since Mapbox is new to me, I was still getting used to the new platform. In class, the exercise really helped with showing me understand how to use the website. I did not use an AI tool to help me create the map, but I used ChatGPT to answer questions about where specific tools were on the website. I also asked questions like how to improve the design with the knowledge I already have.
